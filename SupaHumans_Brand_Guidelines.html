WIP Foundation

What the design has to prove.

Every visual choice earns its place by making the brand feel more credible, not more decorated. The system is confident, clean, and evidence-first — the opposite of hype.

Positioning

Proof over Promise

We show the work, not the pitch. Design leans on real proof — outcomes, faces, specifics — and skips the stock-photo optimism.

Voice

Sage + Caregiver

Knowing and grounded, protective of the people we serve — with a rebel's willingness to name what's broken. Warm, never cute. Direct, never cold.

Category

Talent intelligence

Not a staffing site. A platform brand — the Hiring Brain™ for private practice. The visuals should feel like software, not an agency.

Brand archetypes

Two primary archetypes carry the brand, with a third used as seasoning. The Sage knows; the Caregiver protects; the Rebel refuses the theater. Keep the blend roughly 45 / 40 / 15 — lead with wisdom and care, let the rebel land only when it's earned.

The words are downstream of one idea: proof beats promise. This is the angle, the positioning, and the message architecture the visual system exists to carry — drafted for review.

Mission & Vision

To become the premier healthcare BPO while strengthening 4,444 families in the Philippines within the next 10 years.

Mission · why we exist

To build and strengthen families across the globe by bridging the gap between talented virtual assistants and private practice owners.

The mission is the everyday purpose; the vision is the measurable 10-year destination. The specific figure (4,444) is a proof point in itself — set it in IBM Plex Mono wherever it appears.

Practice owners don't have a hiring problem. They have a seeing problem — they can't tell who can actually do the work until the wrong hire has already cost them. SupaHumans replaces the theater with proof, so the right operator is obvious before Day One.

For private-practice owners who can't afford a wrong front-office hire, SupaHumans is the talent intelligence platform that places operators proven to do the work — not people who interview well. Unlike staffing agencies and VA marketplaces that sell resumes and hope, we lead with proof, match on evidence, and stand behind the fit from Day One™.

01 Logo

The mark.

The glyph is a "U" resolving into an upward-right arrow — the Assistantly submark and the Virtual Rockstar mark merged into one forward motion. It reads as growth, momentum, and moving into the future of hiring.

See the mark come together

Construction

Left: the glyph, used alone for avatars, favicons, app icons, and tight spaces. Right: the primary lockup — glyph + wordmark in fixed proportion. This is the master artwork; don't retype, respace, or rebuild it — use the supplied files.

Clear space & minimum size

Keep clear space equal to half the glyph height on all sides. Nothing — type, edges, other logos — enters this zone.

24px · glyph min
16px · favicon
120px · lockup min width

Below these sizes, use the glyph alone. Never render the wordmark so small it loses its counters.

Colorways — the only approved versions

The full lockup runs one of five ways only: three solid (whole lockup in dark pine, dark grey, or white) or two mixed (lime glyph with the wordmark in dark grey or white). No other color combination is permitted.

Solid — one color

Dark pine — #183A33, default on light.

Dark grey — #333333, neutral / print.

White — on pine, ink, or photography.

Mixed — lime glyph + wordmark

Lime glyph + dark-grey word — on light.

Lime glyph + white word — on pine / dark.

Misuse
✕ Don't invent new color combos (e.g. amber glyph).
✕ Don't put lime on the word or swap the mix.
✕ Don't stretch or distort the lockup.
✕ Don't rotate or tilt the mark.
✕ Don't add shadows, outlines, or effects.
✓ Do use an approved version with clear space.

Special accents — used rarely

Amber (#B45309) and the handwritten Caveat script are not core palette colors — they're special accents that appear only in rare, high-signal moments: a status avatar on a notification, a single hand-drawn aside. Treat them as seasoning; most surfaces never use them at all.

Body — Inter Light

Being seen is structural.

Most processes make you feel chosen — warm words around a decision made for reasons you'll never know. We built the opposite. Every operator arrives with proof of the work, matched by the Hiring Brain™ on the dimensions that actually predict fit, so the right person is obvious before Day One. That is what it means to be seen: not flattered, but understood.

Inter Light is the reading weight — neutral, highly legible, and quiet enough that it never competes with Neue Montreal in the headlines. Body runs Light 300 at 16–18px, 1.6 line-height; step up to Medium 500 only for inline emphasis and links. Captions and small print switch to Neue Montreal Light.

Data & special items — IBM Plex Mono

Anything that is a number or a signal goes in Plex Mono: scores, match times, counts, timestamps, code, and the occasional special callout. The monospacing makes figures line up and feel measured, which reads as proof. This is also where the Sage comes alive visually in the brand — but reserve it for the moments where verified knowledge, the "something we know that they don't," is essential.

For feature moments and product UI, icons sit in a rounded tile — lime line on a faint lime-bordered tile over pine (the treatment used across the site), or pine on lime-soft for light surfaces. One color per icon, always.

Usage: one color per icon — pine on light, white or lime on dark, lime inside the tile. 1.8 stroke, round caps, minimum size 20px. Don't two-tone them, add shadows or gradients to the icon itself, recolor half an icon, or mix in stock icons from another set.

06 Layout

Room to breathe.

Generous whitespace is a brand feature, not empty space. Content sits on a max width around 1080–1200px, sections separated by hairline rules, and the eye is guided by one clear hierarchy per view.

Max width

~1080px content column, 32px page gutters, collapsing to 20px on mobile.

Section rhythm

96px vertical padding on desktop, hairline rgba(16,32,28,.10) dividers.

One hero thesis

Each page opens with a single, characteristic statement — the proof, not a stack of features.
